## 1. CONTEXT AND STARTING POSITION

### What the next 90 days must prove

This is not an organizational listening phase — the product is live, the strategy is complete, and the market doesn't wait. The 90-day mandate is singular: **convert the strategy into the first $5K MRR**.

Every analysis is done. SWOT, personas, competitors, pricing, GTM, KPIs — all complete. The risk is no longer strategic ambiguity. The risk is **execution drag**: having a complete strategy and failing to ship the assets required to make it work. The queue has 50+ action items. Without a forcing function, they will sit there.

The 90-day plan converts the queue into a sequenced, time-boxed execution with clear milestones and a Day 90 checkpoint.

### Top 3 traps for a solo founder at this stage

**Trap 1 — Strategy completeness as a substitute for execution.**
The strategy work done in the last week is exceptional. The trap is doing more strategy (more analysis, more planning, more refinement) instead of shipping the 7 unbuilt GTM assets. The next strategy session should be scheduled for Day 60, not Day 7.

**Trap 2 — Building features instead of acquiring customers.**
The queue has product items (CSV upload, Pro Compliance tier, bulk credits) and GTM items (/compliance page, RapidAPI listing, outreach emails). At $0 MRR, GTM items have higher ROI than product items. The CSV upload can wait until there is evidence Ops Owen is trying to convert. The /compliance page cannot wait — Compliance Claire is the highest-ACV persona and she has no landing page to convert on.

**Trap 3 — Waiting for perfect before shipping.**
The /compliance landing page does not need to be beautiful. The outreach email does not need to be A/B tested before sending batch 1. The RapidAPI listing does not need perfect copy. Ship at 80%, measure, iterate. Every week the landing page doesn't exist is a week of zero Claire conversions.

## 3. PHASE 2 — DAYS 30–60: FIRST REVENUE AND ITERATION

**Goal:** Reach $2,500 MRR. Learn which channel produced the first customers. Double down on it. Fix the first product gaps that are blocking conversions.

### Initiatives (maximum 3)

**Initiative 1 — Scale the channel that worked in Phase 1**

By Day 30, at least one channel will have produced a paying customer or a clear signal. The action is not to add new channels — it is to scale the one that worked.

- If RapidAPI/HN produced customers → write 2 more SEO pages, post on dev.to, submit to developer newsletters (TLDR, Bytes.dev)
- If compliance outreach produced customers → send batch 3 (30 more targets), ask the first customer for a 2-sentence testimonial, add it to the /compliance page
- If neither worked → run the Day 30 war room protocol before adding new channels

**Initiative 2 — Ship Pro Compliance tier ($499)**

If even one Claire persona has replied to outreach, the $499 tier needs to exist before the sales conversation closes. This is 1 day of work: Stripe price ID, SLA PDF, add to /compliance landing page. Ship it by Day 35.

**Initiative 3 — Fix the activation funnel**

By Day 30, the analytics endpoint will show the signup → first call rate. If it's < 40%, the welcome email or docs are the bottleneck. Fix before adding more signups to the top of the funnel.

Likely fixes:
- Welcome email day-1: add a 30-second curl command that works without any setup
- Docs: add a "5-minute quickstart" section at the top (not buried in the full reference)
- Free tier: make the enrichment fields visible in the response (with null values) so Dana can see what she's missing before upgrading

### Operating rhythm changes

**Start:**
- Weekly 30-minute KPI review every Monday (from KPIs plan)
- Daily 2-minute pulse check (3 numbers: signups, API calls by tier, MRR)
- Log every customer conversation — what they asked, what objection they raised, what made them upgrade

**Stop:**
- Strategy analysis sessions until Day 60 (strategy stack is complete — executing it now)
- Building product features that no current customer has requested

**Change:**
- Shift from "build" mode to "sell and iterate" mode — every engineering hour should either fix a conversion blocker or build a KPI measurement tool

### Day 60 milestone target

**$2,500 MRR** — 5–10 paying customers. NRR data beginning to emerge (first cohort is 30 days old). At least one compliance customer and at least one dev customer — two channels validated.

## 5. RISK MAP

### Risk 1 — GTM assets shipped but no conversions (message-market mismatch)

**Early signal:** RapidAPI live 14 days, < 5 signups. 15 outreach emails sent, 0 replies. HN post < 20 upvotes.

**Preventive action:** Before shipping any asset, validate the copy with the sharpest version of the value prop: "FEMA flood zone + census tract, one call, $249/mo, no sales call." If the copy on the asset doesn't lead with this, rewrite it before shipping.

**Response if triggered:** Do not add more channels. Interview 5 people in the target ICP (LinkedIn cold message: "building a US address validation product, would you spend 15 min giving me feedback?"). Find out what they actually care about before rewriting the message.

---

### Risk 2 — First customers sign up on free tier and never upgrade (activation failure)

**Early signal:** 30+ free signups in 30 days, $0 MRR, < 30% make a second API call.

**Preventive action:** Ship the welcome email drip by Day 21 (Phase 1, Week 3). Make enrichment fields visible (nulled) in the free tier response so Dana can see what she's missing. Add an explicit upgrade prompt when the daily rate limit is approached (not just when it's hit).

**Response if triggered:** Email every free signup personally. Do not send a template — send a 2-sentence personal email: "You signed up for GeoClear [X] days ago. What were you trying to build?" Five replies will reveal the blocker faster than any funnel analysis.

---

### Risk 3 — Solo founder burnout causing execution stall

**Early signal:** More than 3 consecutive days without shipping anything from the priority list. Strategy sessions replacing execution sessions. Queue growing instead of shrinking.

**Preventive action:** Time-box each asset to a fixed slot. RapidAPI listing = 60 min, no more. /compliance page = half a day, ship at 80%. The queue exists to prevent decision fatigue — pick the top item, do it, check it off.

**Response if triggered:** Cut scope, not quality. If the /compliance page is taking too long, ship a single-page HTML file with the hero copy, a JSON response example, and a CTA link to the existing portal. It does not need to be designed. A plain-text landing page that exists beats a beautiful one that doesn't.
