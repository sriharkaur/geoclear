## 1. METRICS PHILOSOPHY

### Vanity vs. Actionable for GeoClear specifically

| Vanity (avoid leading with these) | Actionable (decision-driving) |
|----------------------------------|------------------------------|
| Total API calls made | API calls per active paying key (engagement density) |
| Free signups | Free → paid conversion rate by cohort |
| "198M addresses in the database" | Address lookup success rate (did the API return a result?) |
| Uptime % (already high, expected) | p95 response latency (what slow users experience) |
| RapidAPI listing views | RapidAPI referral → signup → first API call |
| Outreach emails sent | Outreach reply rate + trial conversion rate |

**The core trap at GeoClear's stage:** Optimising for free signups feels like growth but is not revenue. A free signup who never makes an API call is not a customer — they are a bounce. The actionable signal is the sequence: signup → first API call → enrichment call → upgrade.

### North Star Metric principle

At $0 MRR, two things matter equally: (1) are customers finding the product, and (2) are they paying for it? These are not the same metric. The North Star must reflect *genuine value delivered* — not discovery, not registration, not even API calls. It must be the moment the customer gets the outcome they came for.

For GeoClear, that moment is: **a paying customer successfully receives enrichment data** (flood zone, census tract, or both) in production. That is the outcome they paid for. Everything else is a leading indicator toward it.

## 2. NORTH STAR METRIC

**Name:** Enrichment Calls by Paying Customers (ECPC)

**Formula:** `COUNT(api_enrich calls WHERE key.tier IN ('pro', 'enterprise', 'pro_compliance')) per rolling 30 days`

**Why this reflects genuine value creation:**
- Free and Builder tier customers are testing. Paying customers in production are getting the outcome they paid for.
- An enrichment call means the customer integrated GeoClear into their workflow — they are not just evaluating, they are dependent.
- This metric cannot be gamed by signups, bots, or demo traffic — only paying customers with real workflows drive it.
- It compounds: a customer making 10,000 enrichment calls/mo is deeply integrated and has high switching cost.

**Measurement frequency:** Weekly (too early for daily signal at current volume)

**Owner:** Founder (until first hire)

**Current baseline:** 0 — there are no paying customers yet. First goal: ECPC > 0. Second goal: ECPC growing week-over-week.

**Lagging indicator check:** If MRR is growing but ECPC is flat, customers are paying but not using enrichment — they may be on Builder/Starter tier without upgrading to enrichment. This is a conversion gap, not a success signal.

## 3. THE 7 KEY KPIs

---

### KPI 1 — Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC) by Channel

**Formula:** `Total time/money spent on channel ÷ number of paying customers acquired from that channel`

At founder-led stage with $0 cash marketing spend, CAC is measured in hours. Assign a shadow rate of $100/hr to founder time.

| Channel | Estimated founder hours | Target paying customers (30 days) | Shadow CAC |
|---------|------------------------|----------------------------------|------------|
| RapidAPI listing | 1 hr setup + 1 hr/mo maintenance | 2–3 | $67–$100 |
| HN Show HN | 2 hrs writing + monitoring | 3–5 | $40–$67 |
| Compliance outreach (15 emails) | 4 hrs list + writing + follow-up | 1–2 | $200–$400 |
| SEO pages (2) | 5 hrs writing + GSC setup | 0–1 (30-day lag) | $500+ initially, $0 at steady state |

**Industry benchmark (API/SaaS PLG):** CAC < 1× monthly revenue = healthy. For a $249/mo product, CAC < $249 is the target.

**Downward deviation (CAC rising):** Channel is not converting. Either the message is wrong (test subject lines / listing copy) or the ICP is wrong (wrong target list).

**Upward deviation (CAC suspiciously low):** Early customers may be personal network / easy wins. Validate that the channel is repeatable before scaling it.

**Causal link to North Star:** Lower CAC → more paying customers → more enrichment calls → ECPC grows.

**Measurement:** Track per channel in a simple spreadsheet. Log every signup source (ask in welcome email: "how did you find us?"). Review weekly.

### KPI 2 — Lifetime Value (LTV) by Cohort and Tier

**Formula:** `Average MRR per customer ÷ Monthly churn rate`

**Tier-level LTV estimates (at target churn rates):**

| Tier | Monthly price | Expected monthly churn | LTV |
|------|--------------|----------------------|-----|
| Builder | $49 | 8% | $613 |
| Professional | $249 | 5% | $4,980 |
| Pro Compliance | $499 | 3% | $16,633 |
| Scale | $999 | 3% | $33,300 |

**Industry benchmark (API SaaS):** LTV/CAC > 3× is minimum viable. > 5× is healthy. > 10× is a strong moat signal.

**Cohort lens:** Track LTV by signup cohort (month), not overall average. An improving cohort LTV over time means the product is getting stickier and the ICP is sharpening. A flat or declining cohort LTV means early customers are churning before replacement customers arrive.

**Downward deviation:** Customers churning before 3 months = product is not delivering on the promise. Conduct exit surveys. At < 5 customers, do this manually — email them directly.

**Upward deviation:** LTV rising fast = expansion revenue happening (customers upgrading tiers). This is the best signal in SaaS — capture it in NRR.

**Causal link to North Star:** Higher LTV → deeper integration → more enrichment calls → ECPC grows.

---

### KPI 3 — LTV/CAC Ratio (Sustainability Indicator)

**Formula:** `LTV ÷ CAC`

**Target:** > 3× at 12 months. > 5× by month 18.

**Current state:** Undefined (no customers, no measured CAC). First goal: establish a baseline from first 5 customers.

**Why this is the sustainability indicator:** LTV/CAC is the single number that tells you whether the business model is structurally sound. A company with LTV/CAC of 1.5 is burning money to acquire customers who will never pay back their acquisition cost. A company with LTV/CAC of 8 has a durable business.

**Downward deviation:** Either CAC is rising (channel efficiency falling) or LTV is falling (churn rising). Diagnose which before acting. The fixes are different.

**Upward deviation:** LTV/CAC > 10 means you are under-investing in acquisition. The marginal dollar spent on customer acquisition returns > $10 in LTV. This is a signal to increase GTM spend, not to coast.

**Causal link to North Star:** High LTV/CAC → sustainable growth → more paying customers → ECPC grows.

### KPI 4 — Retention and Churn by Cohort and Segment

**Formula (monthly revenue churn):** `MRR lost to cancellations ÷ MRR at start of month`

**Formula (net revenue retention):** `(MRR start + expansion - contraction - churn) ÷ MRR start × 100`

**Targets:**

| Metric | Early stage target | Healthy SaaS benchmark |
|--------|-------------------|----------------------|
| Monthly revenue churn | < 5% | < 2% (mature) |
| Net Revenue Retention (NRR) | > 100% | > 110% (great), > 130% (exceptional) |
| 3-month customer retention | > 70% | > 85% |

**Segment breakdown matters:** Dev Dana churns faster (moves to different stack, project ends). Compliance Claire has institutional need — she churns when the product fails or her company changes vendors. Track churn separately per tier.

**Downward deviation (churn rising):** At < 10 customers, every churn event needs a direct conversation — not a survey, a phone call. The cause is almost always one of: (a) product gap discovered in production, (b) pricing shock at renewal, (c) internal budget cut. Each has a different fix.

**Upward deviation (NRR > 130%):** Customers are upgrading faster than they churn. This is the signal to build a self-serve upgrade path (in-app prompts, usage-based nudges) so expansion happens without founder involvement.

**Causal link to North Star:** Low churn → retained paying customers continue making enrichment calls → ECPC is stable and growing.

**Measurement:** Stripe webhook events (`customer.subscription.updated`, `customer.subscription.deleted`) already fire. Log tier transitions to calculate expansion and contraction MRR. Review monthly.

### KPI 5 — Activation and Engagement

**The activation funnel for GeoClear:**

```
Step 1: Signup (GET /v1/signup or POST /v1/checkout)
Step 2: First API call (any endpoint)       ← activation gate 1
Step 3: First enrichment call (/api/enrich) ← activation gate 2 (Pro/Compliance only)
Step 4: Second session (return within 7 days)
Step 5: Upgrade trigger (hit rate limit or enrichment limit)
```

**Key activation metrics:**

| Metric | Formula | Target |
|--------|---------|--------|
| Signup → first call rate | signups who make ≥ 1 API call within 24hrs ÷ total signups | > 60% |
| First call → enrichment call rate | users who hit /api/enrich ÷ users who made first call | > 20% (Pro tier) |
| Time to first call | median hours from signup to first API call | < 2 hrs |
| 7-day retention | signups who make a call in days 2–7 ÷ signups who made day-1 call | > 40% |

**Why time to first call matters:** Every hour between signup and first API call is an hour for doubt to set in. If the docs are unclear or the curl example doesn't work, Dana closes the tab and opens SmartyStreets. The welcome email (day 1 in the GTM plan) exists specifically to reduce this gap.

**Downward deviation (low activation):** Most signups never make an API call = onboarding is broken. Check: (a) welcome email delivered? (b) docs page clear enough? (c) does the free tier curl example work in < 30 seconds?

**Upward deviation (high activation, low upgrade):** Users are active but not upgrading. Check: (a) are they hitting the enrichment limit? (b) is the upgrade CTA visible? (c) is the enrichment gate clear (not confusing)?

**Causal link to North Star:** High activation → users integrate the API → enrichment calls begin → ECPC grows.

**Measurement:** Add `first_call_at` timestamp to `api_keys` table. Track via `GET /v1/admin/analytics` (currently in QUEUE.md T1 — elevated priority given KPI need).

### KPI 6 — Revenue Metrics (MRR, ARR, Expansion, NRR)

**Formulas:**

| Metric | Formula |
|--------|---------|
| MRR | SUM of all active subscription monthly charges |
| ARR | MRR × 12 |
| New MRR | MRR from new customers this month |
| Expansion MRR | MRR increase from existing customers upgrading |
| Contraction MRR | MRR decrease from existing customers downgrading |
| Churned MRR | MRR lost from cancellations |
| Net New MRR | New + Expansion - Contraction - Churned |
| NRR | (MRR start + Expansion - Contraction - Churned) ÷ MRR start × 100 |

**Milestones and targets:**

| Milestone | MRR | What it means |
|-----------|-----|---------------|
| Proof of motion | $500 | First 2 paying customers — something works |
| Channel validation | $2,500 | 5–10 customers — repeatable motion emerging |
| PMF signal | $5,000 | NRR > 100%, customers using enrichment in production |
| Scale threshold | $10,000 | Time to hire or increase GTM spend |
| North star | $100,000 | 12-month goal |

**Downward deviation:** MRR flat for 2 consecutive months with active GTM effort = the channel or the message is wrong. Do not add more channels — fix the one that's failing first.

**Upward deviation (MRR spiking):** Validate the source. A single large customer skewing MRR is different from 20 small customers. Concentration risk: if one customer is > 30% of MRR, that customer leaving would be a crisis.

**Causal link to North Star:** MRR growth = more paying customers + upgrades → more enrichment calls → ECPC grows.

**Measurement:** Stripe dashboard is the source of truth. Supplement with `GET /v1/admin/keys/stats` for tier breakdown. Build `GET /v1/admin/analytics` (in QUEUE.md T1) to surface these numbers without manual Stripe pulls.

## 5. MEASUREMENT TRAPS

### Trap 1 — Optimising for free signups instead of paying customers

**How it happens:** Free signups are easy to grow (post on HN, list on RapidAPI). The number goes up. The founder feels like growth is happening. MRR stays at $0.

**The deception:** Free signups are a leading indicator, not revenue. A 10% free-to-paid conversion rate is good SaaS. 90% of signups will never pay — that is normal and expected. Optimising for more free signups without improving the conversion funnel is running faster on a treadmill.

**Defense:** Track signup → paid conversion rate as a primary metric, not signup count. Set a rule: free signup count is only celebrated if the conversion rate is stable or improving.

---

### Trap 2 — Mistaking activity for traction

**How it happens:** 500 API calls made in a day. "We're getting used!" — but all 500 calls are from 2 free tier users testing the demo widget, not from paying customers building real integrations.

**The deception:** API call volume is meaningless without segmentation by tier and intent. 500 calls from free users costs money (Render compute) and creates no revenue. 50 calls from a Professional customer in production is a health signal.

**Defense:** Always break API call metrics by tier. Free calls are a cost center. Paid calls are the business. Report them separately. The ECPC North Star metric is specifically designed to prevent this trap.

---

### Trap 3 — Survivor bias in cohort analysis

**How it happens:** "Our average customer stays for 8 months!" — but the average is computed only on customers who are still active. The customers who churned after month 1 are excluded from the average.

**The deception:** This makes retention look much better than it is. A company with 50% month-1 churn and 90% retention thereafter will show a high average tenure — but half their customers never made it past month 1.

**Defense:** Use cohort retention charts, not averages. Track every cohort from signup to present. The question is not "how long do customers stay?" but "what % of the January cohort is still active in month 3, 6, 12?" If early cohorts have better retention than recent ones, the product is getting worse or the ICP is drifting.
