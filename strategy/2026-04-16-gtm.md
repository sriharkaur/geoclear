## Next Milestone (this GTM plan targets)

**$5K MRR within 60 days** — 3–5 paying Professional customers ($249/mo) or 1 Pro Compliance ($499) + 2 Professional.

Not $100K. Not even $10K. $5K is the proof-of-motion milestone: it means at least one channel works, at least one ICP converts, and the pricing is defensible. Everything before $5K MRR is hypothesis. After $5K, the GTM shifts from finding the channel to scaling it.

## 1. GTM STRATEGY

### Launch Narrative — 3-Act Story

**Act 1 — The problem (what buyers feel right now):**
Every developer building an address-dependent product hits the same wall: SmartyStreets is expensive and doesn't include enrichment. Google Maps doesn't return flood zone or census tract. Stitching three APIs together costs 2 weeks of engineering time and three billing relationships. Compliance teams pay $10K+/yr for flood zone data that should cost $249/mo.

**Act 2 — The moment (what changed):**
FEMA, the US Census Bureau, and the USDOT publish their data as open federal datasets. A modern API can serve all of it — address validation + flood zone + census tract + timezone + RDI — from a single call, at marginal cost near zero. No one had built it for self-serve until now.

**Act 3 — The solution (GeoClear's position):**
198M US addresses. FEMA flood zone. Census tract. Timezone. Residential flag. One API call. Free tier, no credit card. $249/mo for production. No sales call.

---

### Motion Type: PLG-first with targeted SLG overlay for compliance segment

**Recommendation: PLG primary, outbound SLG secondary.**

**Why PLG primary:** The product demonstrates its own value in < 5 minutes. A developer can sign up, make a call, and see `flood_zone: "AE"` in the response before they've read a single marketing page. PLG is the right motion when the product sells itself on first use — and this product does. PLG also scales at near-zero marginal cost via inbound channels (RapidAPI, HN, Google).

**Why SLG overlay for compliance:** Dev Dana finds the product via PLG channels. Compliance Claire does not — she is not browsing RapidAPI. She has a compliance requirement and is looking for a vendor. She responds to a targeted email that leads with "NFIP flood zone, self-serve, $249/mo." Outbound SLG for 15–20 carefully targeted companies is the right motion for this segment. It is not scalable in its current form — it's a manual wedge to get the first 3 compliance customers, who will then provide case studies to fuel the inbound channel.

**Launch success criterion (30-day validation):**
- PLG validated: ≥ 50 free tier signups + ≥ 2 Starter/Builder upgrades from inbound (RapidAPI or HN)
- SLG validated: ≥ 1 reply from 15 compliance outreach emails within 30 days
- Revenue validated: ≥ 1 paying customer at any tier within 30 days

If all three criteria are met, double down on the channel that produced the customer. If zero criteria are met, the message is wrong — not the product.

## 2. SEGMENTATION AND ICP

### Ideal First Customer Profile

The ICP for the first 30 days is not the same as the long-term ICP. The first customers need to convert without a sales cycle, generate a usable testimonial, and ideally tell others.

**ICP Priority 1 — Developer at a seed/Series A startup (Dev Dana)**

- Role: Full-stack engineer, CTO, or "the person who evaluates APIs"
- Company: Address-dependent product in e-commerce, real estate, logistics, or healthcare
- Why they convert without persuasion: Free tier → self-service → upgrade when they hit volume limits. No decision maker required. No procurement. Credit card on file from day 1 of Starter.
- Why they tell others: Developers share tools on HN, Slack, and Twitter when they save significant engineering time. "One API for address + flood zone + census" is a genuine "Show HN" moment for the people who discover it.
- Channel: RapidAPI, HN, Google organic

**ICP Priority 2 — PropTech/fintech compliance PM (Compliance Claire)**

- Role: VP Product, Head of Engineering, or Compliance Manager at mortgage origination, insurance underwriting, or proptech company
- Company: 10–200 employees, has a compliance checklist that includes NFIP or HMDA
- Why they convert without persuasion: The compliance requirement is pre-existing and assigned. GeoClear is the only self-serve option that clears all five bars (FEMA data, census tract, transparent pricing, no sales call, modern API). The decision is easy once she finds the product.
- Why they tell others: Compliance PMs share vendor recommendations in industry Slack groups (PropTech Insider, Mortgage Tech community) and at conferences. One happy customer in mortgage is worth 5 Developer customers in referral value.
- Channel: Direct outbound email, LinkedIn, /compliance landing page

**ICP Priority 3 — Operations manager with CRM cleanup need (Ops Owen) — DEFERRED**

Owen is product-blocked until CSV upload is built. He should not receive outreach until the no-code bulk path exists. Including him in the first 30-day GTM creates expectation he can't fulfill.

## 6. WAR ROOM PLAN B — Failure Scenarios

### Scenario 1 — HN post gets < 10 upvotes, no traffic spike

**Trigger:** Post submitted, 2 hours pass, < 10 upvotes, rank falling.

**Response within 48 hours:**
1. Do not repost (HN penalizes reposts). Move on — HN is one channel, not the only channel.
2. Extract the HN post title and first comment, reformat as a LinkedIn article and dev.to post. Post both within 48 hours.
3. Redirect HN effort allocation (10%) to Reddit (r/webdev, r/programming, r/selfhosted) — same content, different community. Subreddits allow reposts; HN does not.

---

### Scenario 2 — 0 replies from 15 compliance outreach emails

**Trigger:** Day 7 after batch 1 sent, 0 replies, 0 opens.

**Response within 48 hours:**
1. Check deliverability — did emails land in spam? Send a test to a personal Gmail and check spam folder. If deliverability is the issue: switch to a warm outreach tool (Apollo, Instantly) with domain warm-up.
2. If opens exist but 0 replies: subject line is opening, body is not converting. Rewrite body to lead with a question: "Are you currently using a flood zone determination vendor?" — question-first emails get 2× reply rates on cold outreach.
3. If 0 opens: subject line is failing. Test 3 new variants on batch 2: (a) "Quick question about your flood zone workflow", (b) "NFIP compliance — found something relevant", (c) "[Company] + flood zone API".

---

### Scenario 3 — 0 paying customers after 30 days

**Trigger:** Day 30. Signups exist (≥ 10) but $0 MRR.

**Response within 48 hours:**
1. Email every free signup personally: "You signed up for GeoClear last week — what were you trying to build? What would need to be true for you to upgrade?" — 5 replies will tell you more than 5 strategy analyses.
2. Check the conversion funnel: free signup → first API call → enrichment call → upgrade CTA seen → upgrade. Find where drop-off occurs. If most users never make a second API call, the onboarding is broken. If they make calls but never see the upgrade CTA, the enrichment gate needs to be more visible.
3. Reduce Professional price to $149/mo for the first 5 customers ("founding customer" pricing, locked in for 12 months). This is not a permanent price change — it's a social proof purchase. 5 paying customers at $149 is worth more than 0 customers at $249.
