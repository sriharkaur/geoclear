## 1. PRICING POSITION AUDIT

### How the price is currently perceived

**Dev Dana perception:** $49/mo Starter looks cheap — but she notices enrichment is locked behind $249/mo and immediately asks "do I actually need enrichment?" If she only needs validation, she stays on free or Starter. The $249 Pro gate is the right placement — but the gap between $49 and $249 is $200/mo with no middle option. Some Danas who want partial enrichment churn rather than commit to $249.

**Compliance Claire perception:** $249/mo looks almost suspiciously cheap compared to her current vendor (CoreLogic at $10K+/yr, or SmartyStreets which doesn't even offer flood zone). She reads $249 as "probably a startup, probably not enterprise-grade." The price may be *too low* for her — it signals risk, not value. She would pay $499/mo without hesitation if the product had an SLA and a compliance-specific onboarding flow.

**Ops Owen perception:** Metered pricing is confusing without a calculator. He can't put "pay-per-lookup" in a budget request. He needs a flat number. Starter ($49/mo) reads as "too limited for a bulk cleanup project." Pro ($249/mo) reads as "too expensive for a one-time job." The right offer for Owen doesn't exist yet — it should be a bulk pack (e.g., 1M address credits for $199, no monthly commitment).

### Competitor price anchors

| Anchor | Price | Effect on GeoClear perception |
|--------|-------|-------------------------------|
| CoreLogic / flood determination vendors | $10K–$50K/yr | Makes GeoClear Pro ($249/mo = $2,988/yr) look like 90–99% savings for compliance buyers |
| SmartyStreets at scale | ~$500–$2,000/mo for equivalent volume | Makes GeoClear's volume limits look generous |
| Google Maps Address Validation | $17/1,000 calls at volume | 500K lookups/day on GeoClear Pro = effectively unlimited at $249; Google equivalent would be ~$8,500/day |
| FEMA manual flood determination | $3–$15 per determination | Even 1,000 flood lookups/mo at manual rate = $3,000–$15,000; GeoClear Pro = $249 |

**Key insight:** The FEMA comparison is the most powerful pricing anchor GeoClear has and it is not being used anywhere. "$249/mo vs $3–$15 per manual flood determination" should appear on the pricing page.

## 2. VALUE ECONOMICS

### What the customer loses without GeoClear

**Dev Dana — cost of the alternative:**
- SmartyStreets basic: ~$299/mo for 50K lookups (no enrichment)
- Census Geocoder: free but requires separate API integration (4–8 hrs engineering time)
- FEMA NFHL direct: free but requires a second API call, separate caching, error handling (another 4–8 hrs)
- Total: $299/mo + 8–16 hrs engineering = $299 + ~$1,200–$2,400 one-time dev cost

**Compliance Claire — cost of the alternative:**
- CoreLogic flood determination: ~$15/address manual, or $10K–$50K/yr contract
- SmartyStreets: no flood zone — she can't use it for compliance
- In-house manual lookup: 3 hrs/batch of 500 loans at $50/hr loaded labor = $300/batch
- Annual cost at 1 batch/week: $15,600/yr in labor alone

**Ops Owen — cost of the alternative:**
- Returned shipments from bad addresses: $40K/yr estimated (from persona analysis)
- Failed direct mail campaign: 15% undeliverable at $0.50/mail = $750 per 10K-piece campaign wasted
- Manual CRM cleanup: contractor at $25/hr × 40 hrs = $1,000 per cleanup project

### ROI by scenario (Pro tier, $249/mo = $2,988/yr)

| Scenario | Customer type | Annual cost of problem | GeoClear annual cost | ROI |
|----------|--------------|----------------------|---------------------|-----|
| Conservative | Dev Dana replaces SmartyStreets + census integration | $3,588 ($299/mo + $600 dev time) | $2,988 | 1.2× — saves $600/yr |
| Realistic | Compliance Claire replaces manual flood lookup at 1 batch/week | $15,600 labor | $2,988 | 5.2× — saves $12,612/yr |
| Optimistic | Compliance Claire replaces CoreLogic flood contract | $10,000–$50,000/yr | $2,988 | 3.3×–16.7× savings |

**Pricing headroom conclusion:** The realistic ROI for Compliance Claire is 5×. The optimistic case is 16×. A $499/mo Pro-Compliance tier would still deliver 8× ROI in the realistic scenario. GeoClear is underpriced for the compliance segment.

## 3. GOOD / BETTER / BEST MODEL

### Current structure verdict: Starter is an orphan tier

The Starter tier ($49/mo) has no enrichment. It gives Dev Dana 50× more volume than free — but if Dana actually needs enrichment, she has to jump $200/mo to Pro with no intermediate step. This creates a conversion gap. Starters who discover they need enrichment are forced into a high-commitment upgrade or they churn.

**Recommended fix:** Give Starter a limited enrichment taste — enough to demonstrate value, not enough to replace Pro.

---

### GOOD — Free + Starter

**Who it's for:** Dev Dana at the evaluation and early-build stage. Individual developers, side projects, small startups testing feasibility.

**What's included (recommended):**
- Free: 1,000 lookups/day, basic validation fields, `confidence` score, `residential` flag, `timezone`, `fips` — all currently included. Keep as-is.
- Starter ($49/mo): 50,000 lookups/day + **add: 500 enrichment lookups/mo** (census tract + flood zone via `/api/enrich`). This is a "taste" — enough to integrate and demo, not enough to run in production at any real volume.

**Psychological role:** Lowers the free-to-paid conversion barrier. Dana upgrades to Starter for volume, discovers enrichment works in the 500-call trial window, and is now psychologically invested in upgrading to Pro rather than building around the limitation.

**The constraint that pushes to Pro:** 500 enrichment calls/mo is exhausted in < 1 day of production traffic. The moment Dana's integration goes live, she hits the wall and upgrades.

**Pricing:** Free: $0 | Starter: $49/mo — no change needed. Add the 500 enrichment calls.

---

### BETTER — Pro (the volume buyer and the compliance buyer)

**Who it's for:** Growing startups (Dev Dana at scale), PropTech/fintech compliance teams (Compliance Claire), any team running address workflows in production.

**What's included (current):** 500,000 lookups/day, unlimited enrichment (`/api/enrich`), 1,000 req/min. Keep all of this.

**Why this tier outsells the others:** It's the enrichment unlock. Every buyer who has used the Starter enrichment taste has already proven the value to themselves. Pro is the only tier where the product is complete. Free and Starter feel like demos. Enterprise feels like overhead. Pro is the "just works" tier.

**What gap remains vs. Best:** No SLA, no dedicated support, no custom rate limits, no invoice billing, no BAA/data processing agreement (needed by some regulated companies).

**Recommended change — split into two Pro variants:**
- **Pro — Developer** ($249/mo): current feature set, unchanged
- **Pro — Compliance** ($499/mo): same features + 99.9% uptime SLA document, email support SLA (< 4hr response), data source attestation letter (states that flood zone data is sourced from FEMA NFHL), invoice billing option. This tier exists to solve Compliance Claire's procurement objections, not to add features.

**Rationale:** The $499 Compliance tier adds no engineering work — it's a pricing and support packaging decision. But it: (a) doubles revenue from compliance buyers, (b) signals enterprise-grade positioning, (c) gives Claire something to show legal ("we have an SLA").

---

### BEST — Scale + Enterprise

**Who it's for:** Engineering teams at mid-to-large companies with high volume, custom SLA, or data licensing needs (Ops Owen at enterprise scale, insurance carriers, mortgage platforms).

**Scale ($999/mo) — recommended: keep, add one item:**
- Current: 5M lookups/day, unlimited enrichment, 9,999 req/min
- Add: priority support queue (< 2hr response during business hours), custom rate limit on request
- Psychological role: anchor. Makes Pro ($249 or $499) look reasonable. Most buyers who see $999 move to Pro.

**Enterprise (custom):**
- Unlimited, custom SLA, BAA/DPA, dedicated Slack channel, quarterly data source review, white-label option, data licensing
- Entry price: $2,000/mo minimum (currently unspecified — this ambiguity lets large buyers anchor to $999)
- Add to pricing page: "Starting at $2,000/mo" with "Contact us" CTA

**Bulk Credits Pack (new — for Ops Owen):**
- 1M address validation credits, no expiry, no monthly commitment: **$199 one-time**
- 5M credits: $799 one-time
- No enrichment included (enrichment requires subscription — this is intentional to preserve Pro upsell)
- Sold via a "Need to clean a list?" CTA on the pricing page
- This gives Owen a budget-friendly entry point and a path to Pro when he wants enrichment

## 4. PRICING PSYCHOLOGY

### Anchoring Technique 1 — Lead with the FEMA comparison on the pricing page

Current pricing page leads with feature lists. Recommended: add one line above the pricing grid:

> "Manual flood zone determination costs $3–$15 per address. GeoClear Pro includes unlimited flood zone lookups for $249/mo."

This reframes $249 against the alternative cost rather than against other SaaS tiers. For compliance buyers, this anchor makes the purchase trivial.

### Anchoring Technique 2 — Name tiers to sell the middle one

Current names (Free / Starter / Pro / Scale / Enterprise) are functional but generic. Recommended naming to drive Pro conversion:

| Current | Recommended | Why |
|---------|-------------|-----|
| Free | Free | Keep — "Free" is the right name |
| Starter | Builder | Signals active development, not just testing |
| Pro | **Professional** | "Professional" implies "this is what serious teams use" — drives aspirational upgrade |
| Scale | Scale | Keep — "Scale" signals volume, appropriate |
| Enterprise | Enterprise | Keep |

The rename of Starter → Builder + Pro → Professional creates a cleaner story: you start on Builder, you graduate to Professional when you're serious, you move to Scale when you're big.

### Anchoring Technique 3 — The "most popular" badge goes on Professional, not Builder

This is the classic SaaS middle-tier anchor. Visually highlighting Professional ($249) as "Most Popular" or "Recommended" makes it the default psychological choice. Buyers who are uncertain between Builder and Professional default to Professional because social proof reduces risk. Buyers who are comparing Professional and Scale see Professional as the safe, validated choice.

**Current state:** Pricing page has a volume calculator slider. The visual highlight logic should default to landing on Professional when the page loads (not on the cheapest paid tier).

## 5. PRICING EXPERIMENTS

### Experiment 1 — Starter enrichment taste

**Hypothesis:** Adding 500 enrichment calls/mo to the Starter tier ($49) will increase Starter → Pro upgrade rate by 20%+ within 60 days because developers who integrate enrichment in Starter will exhaust the limit and upgrade rather than rip out the integration.

**Metric:** Starter → Pro upgrade rate (baseline: currently 0 paying customers, so measure from first 30 Starters)

**Implementation:** Add `enrichment_monthly_limit: 500` to Starter tier logic in `keys.js`; return 402 with upgrade prompt when limit is hit

**Timeline:** Ship in 1 sprint; measure over 60 days of data

**Kill criterion:** If upgrade rate from enrichment-enabled Starters is not meaningfully higher than non-enrichment paths, remove the taste and revert — the upgrade driver is volume, not enrichment preview

---

### Experiment 2 — Pro Compliance tier at $499

**Hypothesis:** A $499/mo "Pro Compliance" tier with SLA document + support SLA + data source attestation letter will convert compliance buyers who currently hesitate at $249 (too cheap = too risky) and will double ACV per compliance customer.

**Metric:** Conversion rate of Claire-persona outreach emails to $249 vs $499 tier; measure over 30 outreach contacts per variant

**Implementation:** No engineering required. Create the tier in Stripe, write the SLA document (1-page PDF), add "Pro Compliance" as a pricing option on the `/compliance` landing page. A/B the outreach email: half receive $249 link, half receive $499 link.

**Timeline:** Ship pricing + landing page in < 1 day; run outreach over 30 days

**Kill criterion:** If $499 tier gets zero signups after 20 outreach contacts, the problem is not pricing — it's the message or the target list

---

### Experiment 3 — Bulk Credits Pack for one-time cleanup

**Hypothesis:** A one-time "1M address credits for $199" offer will convert Ops Owen — buyers who have a backlog cleanup project but don't want a monthly subscription — generating revenue from a segment that currently converts at zero.

**Metric:** Bulk pack purchases in first 30 days after launch; follow-on subscription conversion rate from bulk buyers (target: 10% convert to Starter or Pro within 90 days)

**Implementation:** Add bulk pack as a one-time Stripe payment product; add "Clean a list?" CTA to pricing page and portal; no enrichment included in bulk pack

**Timeline:** 1 sprint to implement; measure over 60 days

**Kill criterion:** If 0 bulk purchases in 30 days, the CTA placement or framing is wrong — test a dedicated `/bulk` landing page before abandoning the concept
